I built a small Mac screen recorder

It is called Osmo Banana. It is not a grand SaaS product; it is a local recording workbench I made for myself: record, polish, export.

Start from the core REWORK
The core is not “video creation.” It is removing the ten extra minutes before every demo can be shared.
crazywoola (Banana) · Developer Relations @ Dify
why I started

I did not want another screen-recording SaaS.
I wanted one less monthly bill.

ScreenStudio, Canvid, and Loom are all good. I do not need to chase them. My use case is narrower: everyone is on Mac, recording is local, export is local, and the final video goes to chat, social, or a customer thread.

visual polish

I first make the recording look like a finished asset

The captured frame can be padded, rounded, shadowed, and placed on a solid, gradient, blurred-source, image, or animated procedural background. The key: preview and export share the same renderer.

auto zoom

I treat clicks as editing intent

During recording I already have a cursor log. Where clicks cluster is usually where I want the viewer to look, so I stop manually guessing the camera move.
