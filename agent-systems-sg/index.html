Human Judgment as a Workflow Primitive

Human-in-the-Loop 07 / 21
Pause, request, decide, resume
Say: - "This is the basic Human Input loop: execution pauses, a request is sent, a person reviews the context, and the workflow resumes on the selected path." - "That makes the human handoff explicit, trackable, and repeatable." Focus: - Walk through the flow from top to bottom. - Stress that the workflow continues with context, not from scratch. Bridge: - "Once the handoff exists, we can make it much richer than a simple approve-or-reject."

A Complete Interaction Model

Human-in-the-Loop 08 / 21
Rich interaction and uncertainty handling
Say: - "Human-in-the-Loop is not just approve or reject. Real workflows need edits, feedback, rerouting, retries, and time-based fallback." - "This is how you handle uncertainty without breaking the system." Focus: - Mention wait times and fallback branches because they matter in operations. - Highlight that first response closes the request to avoid ambiguity. Bridge: - "With these pieces in place, we can define what mature agent design really looks like."
